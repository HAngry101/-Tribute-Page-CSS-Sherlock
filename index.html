Sherlock Holmes

Here's a time line of Sherlock Holmes life:

  • January 6, 1854 - Sherlock Holmes is born on this date, which has long been held by Holmesians as his birthday.
  • 1877 - As a medical student, Arthur Conan Doyle first meets Dr. Joseph Bell, who is renowned for his amazing powers of observation and serves as the primary inspiration for Sherlock Holmes.
  • 1887 - The first Sherlock Holmes novel, A Study in Scarlet, is published.
  • 1891 - A Scandal In Bohemia', the first Sherlock Holmes short story, is published in The Strand Magazine, making Holmes and Watson household names.
  • 1893 - Wanting to write other things, Conan Doyle kills off Holmes in 'The Final Problem.' His death is mourned by millions of readers.
  • 1901-1902 - Due to popular demand, Doyle brings back Holmes for a one-off novel, The Hound of the Baskervilles.
  • 1903 - Doyle resurrects Holmes for good in 'The Empty House' after receiving many l etters from broken-hearted fans.
  • 1917 - 'His Last Bow', the last chronological Sherlock Holmes story which sees the detective retire, is published
  • 1927 - 'Shoscombe Old Place', the final Holmes story Conan Doyle ever wrote, is published
  • July 7, 1930 - Arthur Conan Doyle dies at the age of 71
